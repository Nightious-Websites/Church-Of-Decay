On the Marrow-Pact

From Valtherion, a Covenant of Ages

Yuntali once bent around the Valtherion Empire. It began as a single clan of arcane sorcerers and became the greatest power the world has known. The arch-mages of Valtherion could throw workings across a thousand miles, and their rites moved the course of rivers.

The concepts that made such workings possible could not be taught or inherited. In time the arch-mages found that their hands still knew the old shapes but their minds could no longer hold them. The Patron offered his covenant then. The Empire would bring him the souls it could no longer shape, and he would shape them in the Empire's service. A portion stayed with him. The rest returned as power.

From that covenant rose the Church. What Valtherion held by crown and law, the Altar holds by contract and rite. The rolls only grow. The Patron's hand has never slackened.

Six Observances

The Litany of Rot

I
De CarneThe flesh is borrowed from the Patron. Return it with interest when he asks.
II
De SilentioSpeak only in the chapel, and only in whispers. What is barely said is what the Patron hears best.
III
De VigiliaKeep a candle burning in the dark hours. A flame that fails is a vow unkept.
IV
De SanguineLeave the red floor alone. What falls on it stays. The Church does not clean its own memory.
V
De OssibusHonour the bone above the flesh. Bone remembers shape long after the body forgets it.
VI
De PactoThe pact is older than your name and deeper than your blood. What is pledged cannot be recanted, and what is taken is not yours to grieve.
